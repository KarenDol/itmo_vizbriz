        Report Classification Levels
      

      
    

    

      

        The following 7 levels define the hierarchy and classification of medical reports in our system. Each level represents a different type of clinical documentation with specific purposes and requirements.
      

      
      

        
        

          

            
1

            
Initial Assessment Reports

          

          

            Purpose: Comprehensive initial patient evaluation and baseline documentation.

            Includes: Initial consultation notes, baseline medical history, preliminary diagnoses, treatment planning documents, and initial risk assessments.
          

        


        
        

          

            
2

            
Diagnostic Reports

          

          

            Purpose: Detailed diagnostic findings and test results.

            Includes: Sleep study results, imaging reports (CBCT, X-rays), laboratory results, diagnostic test interpretations, and specialist consultation reports.
          

        


        
        

          

            
3

            
Treatment Planning Reports

          

          

            Purpose: Comprehensive treatment strategy and implementation plans.

            Includes: Treatment protocols, device specifications, therapy schedules, compliance plans, and patient education materials.
          

        


        
        

          

            
4

            
Progress Monitoring Reports

          

          

            Purpose: Ongoing treatment effectiveness and patient response tracking.

            Includes: Follow-up visit notes, compliance reports, therapy effectiveness assessments, side effect monitoring, and treatment adjustments.
          

        


        
        

          

            
5

            
Compliance & Quality Reports

          

          

            Purpose: Treatment adherence and quality assurance documentation.

            Includes: Compliance tracking reports, device usage statistics, quality metrics, patient satisfaction surveys, and adherence improvement plans.
          

        


        
        

          

            
6

            
Adverse Event Reports

          

          

            Purpose: Documentation of complications, side effects, and adverse events.

            Includes: Incident reports, adverse event documentation, complication tracking, side effect management, and corrective action plans.
          

        


        
        

          

            
7

            
Outcome & Discharge Reports

          

          

            Purpose: Final treatment outcomes and patient discharge documentation.

            Includes: Final outcome assessments, treatment completion reports, discharge summaries, long-term follow-up plans, and outcome analysis reports.
