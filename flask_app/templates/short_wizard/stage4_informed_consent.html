Informed Consent for the Treatment of Sleep-Disordered Breathing with Oral Appliances

Understanding Sleep Breathing Disorders

Snoring and Obstructive Sleep Apnea (OSA) are breathing disorders that occur during sleep due to narrowing or complete blockage of the airway. Snoring results from partial obstruction producing noise; persistent or loud snoring can indicate serious health risks such as high blood pressure.

OSA involves repeated airway collapse that reduces oxygen levels and disrupts sleep. Untreated, it can contribute to excessive daytime sleepiness, irregular heartbeat, hypertension, acid reflux, mood changes, and—in severe cases—cardiac or cerebrovascular events.

Diagnostic Process and Treatment

Before therapy, individuals are typically evaluated through a home sleep test or overnight polysomnography.

Oral appliance therapy works by gently repositioning the jaw and tongue to help maintain an open airway, sometimes improving nasal airflow as well. Clinical studies confirm that oral appliances can reduce snoring and mild-to-moderate sleep apnea, but outcomes vary based on factors such as nasal obstruction, jaw anatomy, and body weight. Follow-up testing is required to confirm therapeutic success.

Possible Complications

Some patients may initially experience:

  • Excessive salivation or dry mouth
  • Temporary jaw joint or muscle discomfort
  • Tooth or gum soreness
  • Mild bite changes upon waking

These effects are generally temporary. In rare cases, longer-term changes in bite alignment or tooth position can occur. Appliances may loosen, fracture, or break; damaged components can pose swallowing or aspiration risks. If discomfort or damage occurs, discontinue use and contact your provider promptly.

Length of Treatment and Monitoring

The appliance is a mechanical device that must be worn nightly for ongoing benefit.

Sleep-disordered breathing may change over time; therefore, regular follow-up visits (at least once every six months) are required to verify fit and monitor oral health.

Even if symptoms improve, repeat sleep testing is periodically recommended to confirm adequate oxygenation and overall treatment effectiveness.

Patient Disclosure

I confirm that I have provided complete and accurate information about my medical history, medications, prior dental work, and any previous sleep disorder treatments. I understand that undisclosed information may affect the safety or effectiveness of this therapy.

Device Care and Responsibility

I agree to clean, store, and maintain my oral appliance as instructed. I will report any discomfort, wear, or damage immediately. I understand that replacement, repair, or adjustment costs may not be covered by my provider.

Data and Record Sharing Consent

I consent to the secure electronic sharing of my diagnostic data, imaging, and treatment progress among my healthcare providers and authorized service partners for purposes of treatment coordination, billing, and insurance submission. All data will be handled in accordance with HIPAA and applicable privacy laws.

Treatment Alternatives

Alternative or adjunctive treatments may include lifestyle modification, weight management, positional therapy, continuous positive airway pressure (CPAP), surgical intervention, or combination therapy. These options have been explained, and I may pursue or refuse them at my discretion.

Uncommon or Serious Complications

Though rare, other possible outcomes include damage to dental restorations, loosening of teeth, root sensitivity, temporomandibular joint dysfunction, soft-tissue irritation, or muscle pain. I understand that such complications may require additional dental or medical treatment.

Emergency or Discontinuation Guidance

I will stop using the appliance and contact my provider immediately if I experience persistent jaw pain, bleeding, dizziness, or other concerning symptoms. I understand that discontinuing therapy without consultation may allow my condition to worsen.

Consent Declaration

By signing below, I authorize treatment of my sleep-disordered breathing using an oral appliance (mandibular advancement device).

I grant permission for necessary oral examinations, imaging, photography, and therapeutic procedures. I acknowledge that no guarantees of success have been made and that outcomes depend on individual factors and adherence to therapy.

I have read and understood this consent form, have had the opportunity to ask questions, and voluntarily agree to treatment.
